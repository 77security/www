## Why AI Security?

The rapid adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Autonomous Agents has created a new attack surface that traditional cybersecurity was never built to handle. Unlike classic software, AI systems are **probabilistic**, not deterministic. This creates unique vulnerabilities where the "code" is actually natural language.



### The New Frontier of Risk
Traditional firewalls look for malicious code; however, in an AI-driven world, a "malicious" input might look like a perfectly normal sentence. 

* **Semantic Vulnerabilities:** Attacks like *Prompt Injection* exploit the way models interpret instructions, turning a trusted assistant into a "confused deputy."
* **Data Integrity:** As models learn from vast datasets, *Data Poisoning* can introduce subtle biases or backdoors that remain hidden until triggered by a specific phrase.
* **Agentic Risk:** When we give AI the power to browse the web or execute API calls, we aren't just deploying a chatbot—we are deploying a user with high-level privileges.
